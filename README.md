Training specialized OpenClaw agents for neuropathology whole-slide image (WSI) analysis. Current skills focus on neuroanatomy, lesion interpretation, tauopathy staging, multimodal reasoning, and scientific-method workflows inspired by Cajal, Alzheimer, and classical observational neuroscience.
The long-term vision is an ecosystem of neuropathology-native AI agents that can assist with research, education, annotation, quality control, differential diagnosis, and eventually large-scale clinicopathologic discovery. The potential here is enormous, but the claws need a great deal of domain-specific training before they become truly useful. Histology is messy, contextual, and deeply dependent on expert observational reasoning.
This repository is an early attempt to encode parts of that reasoning process into modular, inspectable skills and workflows rather than opaque prompts. More skills, datasets, and orchestration pipelines are coming. Follow the project, experiment with the skills, and join us as we try to teach machines how neuropathologists actually think.
